## Overview
This quickstart guide provides educators and students with a rapid understanding of the ROS 2 curriculum module structure, progression, and integration with the overall Physical AI program. It serves as a high-level summary of the five-chapter curriculum that establishes ROS 2 as the robotic nervous system for Physical AI applications.

## Module Structure at a Glance

### The Five-Chapter Progression
1. **Chapter 1**: Introduction to Physical AI - Core concepts and ROS 2 as middleware (5000-7000 words)
2. **Chapter 2**: Embodied Intelligence and Sensors - Perception and grounding in sensors (5000-7000 words)
3. **Chapter 3**: ROS 2 Architecture - Internal design and QoS policies (5000-7000 words)
4. **Chapter 4**: Nodes, Topics, Services, and Actions - Communication patterns (5000-7000 words)
5. **Chapter 5**: ROS 2 Packages and Launch Files - System composition and deployment (5000-7000 words)

### Key Learning Progression
- **Foundation First**: Each chapter builds upon previous knowledge while introducing new concepts
- **Systems Thinking**: All components designed for integration into larger robotic systems
- **Practical Application**: Each chapter includes hands-on labs and real-world examples
- **Capstone Ready**: Content prepares students for integrated capstone system development

## For Educators: Curriculum Pacing

### Chapter Overview
- **Chapter 1** (Weeks 1-3): Physical AI foundations and ROS 2 basics - Essential groundwork
- **Chapter 2** (Weeks 4-6): Sensor integration and perception systems - Critical for understanding embodiment
- **Chapter 3** (Weeks 7-9): Architecture deep-dive - Important for robust system design
- **Chapter 4** (Weeks 10-12): Communication patterns - Core for system integration
- **Chapter 5** (Weeks 13-15): System composition - Essential for capstone preparation

### Prerequisite Relationships
- Students need Chapter 1 knowledge (ROS 2 fundamentals) before proceeding to other chapters
- Chapter 2 builds on Chapter 1 concepts while introducing sensor integration
- Chapters 3-5 require solid understanding of ROS 2 architecture and communication
- All chapters feed into capstone system integration
